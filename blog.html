Technology

Digital technologies have progressed faster than any other breakthrough in history, reaching over half of the world's population in just two decades and altering society. Technology can help to level the playing field through improving connection, financial inclusion, trade access, and access to public services. For example, AI-enabled frontier technologies in the health sector are assisting in the saving of lives, the diagnosis of illnesses, and the extension of life expectancy. Virtual learning environments and distant learning have offered up educational opportunities to students who would otherwise be shut out. Through blockchain-powered platforms, public services are becoming more accessible and accountable, as well as less bureaucratically demanding as a result of AI support. Big data may also help policymakers and programmers create more responsive and accurate policies and programs.


Big Data

The phrase "big data" refers to data collections that are both complicated and large in size. Big data refers to information that may be studied to uncover hidden information such as market trends, undiscovered relationships, patterns, client preferences, and so on. Big data is a term that refers to the process of gathering, cleaning, and analyzing enormous volumes of data for organizations to make educated decisions. Traditional data processing apps cannot handle big data. Big data is no longer novel to businesses and society in the age of information and advanced analytics. Big data has been around for a long time. Organizations produce insights and make well-informed decisions, find patterns, and enhance productivity with big data solutions, it is widely known. Big data, on the other hand, is more than that. Big data offers organizations several benefits and has an influence on enterprises, the workforce, and society.
